Capstone Research

Research topic as part of the Honours component of my degree



Teleoperation Hand Tracking Control of Robotic Hand


For my final year of university study, I am currently completing a self-assigned capstone research project. My project will focus on teleoperation (i.e. remote control) of robot hand gripper using hand tracking technology.

Robotic arms are often teleoperated to perform tasks that are either dangerous or require specific physical capabilities exceeding that of a person. Current common methods of controlling these robots include joysticks, handheld controllers, and manual computer command inputs. Although these methods are adequate, my project will research the potential for a more ergonomic and user-friendly control method for these robotic systems, which could greatly improve their effectiveness. This will be achieved using hand tracking technologies to control a robotic human hand manipulator that can mirror a person’s own hand movements. Communication of hand tracking data over a local or non-local network (internet) will also be researched to support applications where the robot and operator are not in the same location.

Potential technology options to implement this research include the leap motion controller hand tracking device and the InMoov open-source 3D printed robot hand. These are shown in the accompanying figures.
